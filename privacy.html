Privacy Policy

At Ajiva Talks, we respect your privacy and are committed to protecting it. This policy explains what information we collect, how it is used, and the choices you have regarding your data.

Information We Collect

We collect minimal information and only when necessary.

Information you may provide voluntarily

  • Name and email address when you contact us
  • Information included in interview pitches, submissions, or correspondence

Automatically collected information

  • Basic, anonymous usage data such as page views, browser type, and device information
  • This data helps us understand how readers engage with the site and improve the reading experience

We do not collect sensitive personal data.

How We Use Your Information

Your information is used only to:

  • Respond to inquiries or interview requests
  • Communicate regarding editorial matters
  • Improve site performance and user experience

We do not sell, rent, or trade personal information.

Cookies and Analytics

Ajiva Talks may use cookies or lightweight analytics tools to understand site traffic and reading behavior. These tools collect non-identifiable, aggregated data and do not track users across the web.

You can disable cookies in your browser settings at any time.

Third-Party Services

We may use trusted third-party services (such as analytics or email providers) strictly for operational purposes. These services are required to handle data responsibly and in compliance with applicable privacy standards.

Ajiva Talks is not responsible for the privacy practices of external websites linked from our pages.

Data Retention

We retain personal information only for as long as necessary to fulfill its purpose or comply with legal obligations. You may request deletion of your data at any time.

Your Rights

You have the right to:

  • Request access to your personal data
  • Request correction or deletion of your data
  • Withdraw consent for communication at any time

To make a request, contact us at: hello@ajivatalks.in

Children's Privacy

Ajiva Talks is not intended for children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect personal data from minors.

Changes to This Policy

This Privacy Policy may be updated periodically. Any changes will be reflected on this page with a revised effective date.
