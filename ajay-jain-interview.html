Ajay Jain — The Visionary Behind Kunzum & India’s Travel-Literary Movement

Founder of Kunzum Café • Author of 11 Books • Photographer • Creative Entrepreneur

Ajay Jain is a name that resonates with creativity, entrepreneurship, and a relentless passion for literature. He is not only the founder of Kunzum Café, a beloved community space, but also the author of 11 print books and 50+ e-books. His journey continues to inspire travelers, writers, and young entrepreneurs who dream boldly.

Exclusive Interview

1. Can you tell us about your journey as an entrepreneur and how you came up with the idea for Kunzum Café and bookstores?
Ajay Jain: In some ways, my life is a story / saga in itself. I have had careers in technology, sports management and media sectors and hold degrees / certifications in mechanical engineering, management, journalism and photography.

I took to writing books in 2007 – while my initial focus was on travel, I expanded the same to write on personal development, marketing and fiction too. And this is how Kunzum the brand was born.

It was the name of my travel blog... and I set up the Kunzum Travel Café in 2010 to serve as a gallery for my photography and books. It grew into a community space built around conversations.

The pandemic led to a transition, and today Kunzum Books is expanding as a chain of bookstores built around community, curation and creativity.
2. Kunzum Café is known for its unique “pay what you like” model. What inspired it?
Ajay Jain: The idea was to eliminate barriers and build community. We wanted guests to feel at home. The concept took off far beyond expectations and became a major reason Kunzum is loved.

Now, coffee and tea are complimentary — but only if you buy a book. You can’t buy the coffee. You get it only with a book. It’s a fun, culture-building idea.
3. You’ve ventured into both hospitality and retail. How do you balance both?
Ajay Jain: We are not in hospitality anymore. Only retail. And I continue to focus on my writing and photography.
4. Your book “NIKITA” received acclaim. What inspired it?
Ajay Jain: My interest in human behaviour, relationships and reflection. Nikita is a memoir based on the true story of someone I know. Writing it was an exercise in understanding life.
5. You’ve written 11 print books and 50+ e-books. What is your writing process like?
Ajay Jain: I write in spurts. When I sit down, I finish extremely fast. But there are long periods of thinking, reflecting and procrastinating in between.

I maintain stacks of handwritten notes — ideas, reflections, insights. When it’s time to write, these become my foundation.
6. In the era of digital publishing, what motivates you to continue publishing print books?
Ajay Jain: I have purchased three Kindles and two iPads, yet I have not read even five e-books. For me, reading has to be ink on paper. That's how I connect with a story.

Even in the USA, e-book and audiobook penetration is less than 20%. The evidence says readers still prefer print.

We are on the cusp of a reading renaissance — a new wave of people rediscovering books.
7. Kunzum Café has been a hub for travelers and writers. What role has it played in fostering community?
Ajay Jain: I meet creative people all the time who say they attended events, launches or simply enjoyed a quiet cup at Kunzum.

Although I traveled a lot earlier, I’m still amazed at how many people carry memories of Kunzum with them.
8. How do you manage to excel as both an entrepreneur and author?
Ajay Jain: It begins with a zest for living — being creative, experimental, rejecting toxicity, and constantly learning.

But this must be backed by hard work and discipline. When you mix all this, you can wear multiple hats without being a jack of all trades.
9. How has travel influenced your writing and business?
Ajay Jain: Travel — not tourism — is the greatest school. Books are the other.

When you travel with an open mind, life widens. You gain new perspectives, fresh ideas, and deeper understanding.

Travel keeps you youthful, curious, humble — and it reveals paths you never knew existed.
10. What trends do you see emerging in the book retail industry?
Ajay Jain: In retail, the differentiator is experience. At Kunzum, that includes:

• Community • Curated book selections • Book clubs • Events like workshops, talks, and storytelling • Activity corners • Reviews & recommendations • Complimentary coffee (only when you buy a book!)
We’re building a culture around books.
11. Any upcoming projects you’re excited about?
Ajay Jain: We’re building a large community ecosystem through the Kunzum Book Club — in schools, corporates, neighborhoods.

These clubs will host discussions, workshops, author sessions, competitions and more — all aimed at promoting reading as a culture.
12. What legacy do you hope to leave?
Ajay Jain: I try to live meaningfully and creatively. If my journey inspires others to live to their full potential, that is reward enough.

People have often told me they made bold choices because of something they saw in my work. That stays with me.
13. How do you plan to foster innovation at Kunzum and engage younger audiences?
Ajay Jain: I’ve experimented a lot with format, layout, and presentation. One thing is clear — readers on the internet skim.

So the content must be: • Short paragraphs • Clear subheads • Conversational tone • Visually easy
We will add youth debates, startup journeys, creative showcases — a platform where young voices truly get space.
14. How will you ensure international coverage remains a priority at Kunzum?
Ajay Jain: Patience. No rushing complex geopolitical topics.

We fact-check deeply, cross-check sources globally, and avoid publishing until we are confident of accuracy.
Even in complex global conflicts, truth must be handled responsibly.
15. How do you measure the success of Kunzum under your leadership?
Ajay Jain: Success is when readers feel connected.

Parents, children, corporate leaders, authors — when they write saying Kunzum made them read more, learn more, think more… that’s success.
In the coming years, I want the platform to become even more user-friendly, interactive, and open for large-group conversations.
16. Can you share about your journey exploring tribal rituals and archaeology in Chhattisgarh?
Ajay Jain: Rural journalism is the heart of India — 64.61% of Indians live in villages, yet coverage is barely 0.08%.

Who checks if policies are implemented? Who documents lost temples? Who studies tribal culture without stereotypes?

I worked 16–21 hours daily — meeting villagers, studying archaeology, exploring tribal psychology, interviewing artisans and royals, driving across mountains and riverbeds.
India’s beauty lies in its details — and I want the world to see those details.
17. What unique tribal practices did you discover in Chhattisgarh?
Ajay Jain: The tribes of Bastar practice animism — their gods have no fixed form.

Highlights include: • World’s longest-running festival • No Ravana Dahan — they consider themselves of the Asura clan • Dormitory system where boys & girls understand compatibility before marriage • Ancient food traditions like red ant chutney, bamboo-shot Aamat • Deep tattoo (Godna) culture
More is revealed in the book — I must leave some surprises for readers.
