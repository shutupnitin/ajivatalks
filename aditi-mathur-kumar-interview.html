Aditi Mathur Kumar — Operation Payback, Veer Naris & Life as an Army Wife

By Ajiva Talks
•
Author Interviews / Military Fiction
•
— min read

Aditi Mathur Kumar is an author, devoted mother, advertising professional and proud Army wife. Her latest novel Operation Payback follows Meera — a Veer Nari who becomes the architect of a daring plan after losing her PARA SF husband to a terror attack. Below, Aditi discusses authenticity, research, resilience and what the story aims to give readers.

Quick facts

Works: Soldier & Spice; Operation Payback

Perspective: Army life, Veer Naris

Role: Author, Army Wife

Donate

1. Can you tell us about the inspiration behind "Operation Payback"? What led you to explore the story of Meera, a Veer Nari, and her journey towards seeking justice and honor?
Aditi Mathur Kumar: Operation Payback is the story of a Veer Nari (war widow) who decides to join the Army and serve the nation after her PARA SF commando husband is killed in action in a terror attack in Kashmir – and the story covers her enthralling and inspiring journey of being the first lady officer in combat, discovering her inner strength and learning about true heroism while getting payback. The story is inspired by real life heroes, a few Veer Naris who joined the Army.

As an Army Wife myself, I often found myself wondering what motivates a Veer Nari to join the Army after — the profession where her late husband gave his life at the line of duty. I wanted to understand the motivation and the drive behind the decision. Then I met two such brave women who walked this path — and I understood the purpose that is larger than life. Operation Payback is my tribute to not just the Indian Army, but to all Veer Naris who make the courageous choice of joining the Army and dedicating their life to the nation’s safety. I applaud their unshakable courage and I salute their spirit via this story.
2. The protagonist, Meera, undergoes a significant transformation from a grieving widow to a determined soldier seeking payback. How did you approach depicting this evolution, and what challenges did you face in portraying such a complex character arc?
Aditi Mathur Kumar: To write about a grieving character is never easy. My approach was to keep it real and raw, so that it evokes emotions in the readers while simultaneously telling them that this is a reality of every army wife’s life — I showed Meera’s evolution from an engineer to an army wife to a war widow. If I’m being honest, it took a toll on me emotionally because I personally know fellow army wives who have become Veer Naris and had their entire lives changed in a second. It’s scary, but I tried to stay true to the emotion and built the story with authenticity.
3. "Operation Payback" delves into themes of bravery, heroism, and resilience. How did you ensure that these themes were effectively conveyed throughout the narrative?
Aditi Mathur Kumar: While writing this story, I lived as several characters in the book and stayed true to them and only their journey — that is how I tried to ensure that various POVs, emotions and themes were conveyed in a way that’s as real as it gets, unbridled by the need to abide by social perceptions or diplomacy. I feel strongly about telling army stories, and that passion helped me in writing about demanding and complex themes of heroism, bravery and resilience. I wish I was able to do justice to them.
4. The novel is described as "tech-led" and "strategic." Could you elaborate on how technology and strategy play a role in Meera's quest for justice?
Aditi Mathur Kumar: Technology is at the core of this story — as is strategy. Along with active practice, the army invests a lot of time and effort in planning and strategy, and I wanted to showcase that aspect too. In Meera’s story, both technology and strategy play a role in her accomplishing the ultimate payback. While technology enabled her to decipher signals, a smart strategy helped her and the entire team in executing their plan.
5. Meera's decision to join the Indian Army to honor her late husband's legacy sets the stage for the novel's plot. How did you balance the emotional depth of her personal journey with the high-stakes action of the story?
Aditi Mathur Kumar: The urge to give in to the catharsis of Meera’s emotions when that one dreadful phone call came was immense. I wanted to go deeper into her emotions of loss, grief, helplessness and anger to do justice to her character. However, I enjoy writing action scenes that move the story in a show-not-tell manner, so I tried to marry the two. To tell the story correctly, neither could be compromised. I did end up writing more about Meera’s emotional journey than appears in the final book because during editing I balanced it out. Like every author I know says, it is easy to write but tough to edit your own work — and I felt that here more than with my previous books.
6. As an author who has explored the lives of defense families in your previous work, how did your personal experiences and research inform the portrayal of military life and the challenges faced by Veer Naris in "Operation Payback"?
Aditi Mathur Kumar: I am an Army Wife, and I like to create characters based on real-life inspiration. Being part of the army fraternity has given me rich experiences — life as a military spouse, the journey of army kids, emotions of army parents and more. This is what I used as fuel for previous books like Soldier & Spice. For Operation Payback, a lot of research on terrain, weapons, and ops strategy was required — I read a lot, spoke to many people and put my head down to get everything right. While the story and characters are fictional, the setting is as authentic as it gets. I am very proud of this.
7. Meera's journey is not just about seeking revenge but also about delivering justice for the nation. How did you navigate the balance between Meera's personal motivations and the larger patriotic duty she feels compelled to fulfill?
Aditi Mathur Kumar: While there is immense satisfaction in Meera’s personal revenge, the idea of heroism is never selfish. I wanted to reflect Ranvijay’s inspiring ideology about what makes a true hero, like every soldier in our forces. Heroism can mean different things to different people, and I depicted the heavy conundrum in Meera’s head in the second half of the story. The end needed to be worthy of a true hero, showing courage that places the larger good before one’s own need. That is what true valour looks like, in my head. When readers tell me the end gave them goosebumps and tears, it makes me proud.
8. The title "Operation Payback" suggests a calculated and strategic approach to seeking justice. Can you discuss how Meera's plan unfolds throughout the novel without revealing any spoilers?
Aditi Mathur Kumar: The title is inspired by Operation Vijay and is my small homage to every soldier who was a part of it, and their families. Meera’s payback is bigger than plain revenge — that is the beauty of the ending. Once a story becomes a book and reaches readers, it becomes everyone’s story. Many readers appreciated that payback was delivered not only by strategy and help from other characters/organizations, but also by Meera’s passion and, yes, a little bit of luck. I’ll request readers to buy a copy and read the story so I can learn how the plan made them feel.
9. What do you hope readers will take away from "Operation Payback," particularly in terms of its themes of empowerment and resilience?
Aditi Mathur Kumar: The story has many takeaways — everyday heroism of our soldiers, the resilience of the main character, unexpected support and unadulterated trust. If I had to pick one theme it would be resilience for girls. I wish readers find the story inspiring. I hope it makes young girls and boys want to join the army. I hope readers realize that when a soldier gets ready to do duty at the borders, an entire family makes sacrifices. And I hope everyone who reads this understands that she who waits, also serves.
10. Lastly, what message or lesson do you believe Meera's story imparts to readers, especially those facing their own adversities or seeking to overcome loss and grief?
Aditi Mathur Kumar: The biggest lesson is to rise above your circumstances and be resilient. We all heard growing up that luck favours the brave, and this story is testament to it. Be brave because, as my favourite artist Piyush Mishra says in the poem, “Yuddh hee toh veer ka praman hai”. Jai Hind.
