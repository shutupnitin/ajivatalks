Resilience, Partition & Human Spirit — A Conversation with Priya Hajela

Literature · Author Interviews · Ajiva Talks

Priya Hajela’s writing is deeply influenced by her rich tapestry of experiences — a testament to the resilience she has cultivated as a mother, an immigrant navigating cultural transitions, and a professional thriving in the high-stakes worlds of telecom and IT. Born in Ludhiana, India, Priya’s early years were shaped by time spent in northern Indian boarding schools, something that likely instilled independence and adaptability early on.

Her life took a transformative turn when she moved to the United States, where she pursued higher studies and built a career. Nearly two decades later, she returned to India with a broadened worldview, ready to explore her creative side. Her decision in 2014 to challenge herself to write a novel-length piece in one month was the spark that ignited her literary journey. What started as an experiment turned into a passion, leading her to pursue an MFA in Creative Writing from Goddard College in Vermont.

Her debut novel, Ladies’ Tailor, published by HarperCollins India, blends history, resilience, and the intricacies of human relationships — a reflection of both her experiences and her curiosity about identity, belonging, and survival.

1. Can you please give us a brief description about your book “Ladies’ Tailor”?
Priya Hajela: Ladies’ Tailor is a story about the human spirit in all its glory. It is about losing everything and rebuilding, about loss and friendship, about love and tragedy. It begins with partition and the effects of that ill-conceived event on masses of humanity but it quickly moves into recovering and rebuilding. Gurdev, the main protagonist, is a strong character.
